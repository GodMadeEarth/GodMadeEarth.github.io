I'm Timothy

 I began learning around 5th-6th grade to code for two big reasons, people could possibly paid do it and I was intrigued by the incredible things you could create through lines of code. This is what lead me to learning Python at a early age and with slight influence I decided to create a card game. Not a ordinary card game but a card game about ninjas throwing cards at each other.

Many fake terminals, made in Python, later on down the line I started playing with Godot 3 and doing game jams starting in 2021. Over the next few years I would create side projects like virtual circuit boards or sprite stacks until Godot 4 came out.

By this time I've programmed off and on for around 5-7 years but still had no clue of my worth. During my time in college, in Python I created tools to transfer data to datasheets for data entry and almost created a sudo language interpreter. I stopped due to a single equal sign being used for comparing and setting values; That is a crime!

Around this time I put the pieces together that I had ADHD which explained my amazing talent with programming and poor talent elsewhere. Eventually I ran across an opportunity to be paid to code in Godot 4 and jumped at the opportunity and here I am now.
